🧩 Functional Needs (what users need to do)

These come primarily from the dominant “JTBD” (jobs-to-be-done) and pain patterns:

1. Faster, clearer decision-making
Compare vendor quotes quickly with full landed cost visibility
Access side-by-side comparisons (price, freight, quality, reliability)
Reduce time spent consolidating data from multiple sources
2. End-to-end procurement speed
Move from final quote → approved PO without delays
Reduce procurement cycle time and approval bottlenecks
Minimize manual follow-ups across stakeholders
3. Supply risk visibility
Detect supply disruptions early
Identify vendor reliability risks before ordering
Track lead times and variability proactively
4. Quality issue resolution
Resolve quality exceptions quickly
Avoid repeated manual coordination between teams and vendors
Maintain traceability of defects and corrective actions
5. Compliance without friction
Ensure policy adherence automatically
Avoid manual documentation and audit prep work
Have clear approval trails and justification records
6. Data-backed negotiation
Use benchmarks and historical data in negotiations
Understand fair pricing and cost breakdowns
Improve bargaining power with evidence
7. Total cost visibility (TCO)
See true cost beyond unit price (freight, defects, delays)
Make decisions based on overall business impact, not just price
❤️ Emotional Needs (how users want to feel)

These are driven by the dominant emotions in the dataset.

1. In control (vs. “blocked”)
Users frequently feel blocked
Need: Clear workflows, fewer dependencies, autonomy in execution
2. Time-secure (vs. “pressed for time”, “rushed”)
Strong sense of urgency and overload
Need: Speed, automation, and reduced manual effort
3. Confident in decisions (vs. “concerned”)
Anxiety around making the wrong vendor or cost decision
Need: Reliable data, predictive insights, and transparency
4. Less burdened (vs. “burdened”, “dependent”)
Heavy reliance on others (finance, QA, vendors)
Need: Streamlined coordination and reduced back-and-forth
5. Calm and proactive (vs. “reactive”, “pressured”)
Current mode is reactive firefighting
Need: Early warnings, planning tools, and foresight
6. Friction-free experience (vs. “frustrated”, “irritated”)
Frustration from fragmented systems and repeated manual work
Need: Seamless, integrated workflows
🔑 Key Insight
Functional needs are about speed, visibility, and automation
Emotional needs are about control, confidence, and relief from pressure

In short:
👉 Users don’t just want better procurement tools
👉 They want to stop firefighting and feel in control of outcomes
